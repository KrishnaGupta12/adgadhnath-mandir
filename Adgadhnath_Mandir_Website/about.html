In the peaceful and spiritual Sohagi Hills of Rewa district, Madhya Pradesh, the Adgadnath Mahadev Temple is an ancient and sacred site. This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, who is believed to have manifested here in a miraculous and divine form.

to local legends and elders, Lord Shiva appeared in the form of a coconut-shaped idol, emerging naturally from the roots of a Tendu tree. This miraculous event is believed to have occurred around Samvat 1740, and the site later became the foundation for the temple.

It is said that King Randhir Singh Sengar of Garhwa Naigarh received a divine vision from Lord Shiva in a dream, instructing him to build a temple at this location. The king followed the divine command, constructed the temple, and performed the Pran Pratishtha (consecration ceremony) with full rituals.
